Chè Đậu Trắng

White Bean Sweet Soup

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
15 min + soak
Cook Time
45 min
Servings
8–10
Category
Chè

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 3 cans white beans (đậu trắng), drained and rinsed
  • 1¼ cups sticky rice (gạo nếp), rinsed
  • 2 cups sugar, or to taste
  • 5⅓ cups water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Coconut cream for drizzling (optional)

Phần lớn hơn · Larger Batch

  • 3 cans white beans
  • 2 cups sticky rice
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 7 cups water

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Rinse the sticky rice several times until the water runs clear. Soak in water for at least 1 hour if you have time — this helps it cook softer and faster.
  2. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil. Add the drained sticky rice. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is completely soft and the mixture turns creamy.
  3. Drain and rinse the canned white beans. Add them to the pot with the cooked sticky rice. Stir gently to combine — be careful not to mash the beans too much. Mẹ liked the beans to stay whole so each spoonful had that nice soft bite.
  4. Add sugar and a pinch of salt. Stir until the sugar is fully dissolved. Taste and adjust sweetness — Mẹ always said "thêm đường từ từ" (add sugar slowly), you can always add more but you can't take it back.
  5. Simmer everything together for another 5–10 minutes so the flavors meld. The chè should be thick but still pourable — add a little more water if it gets too thick.
  6. Serve warm in small bowls, drizzled with coconut cream if you like. This chè is also lovely chilled on hot days.
