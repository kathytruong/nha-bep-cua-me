Bò Kho

Vietnamese Beef Stew

From Mẹ — Oanh

Category
Món Mặn

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 3 lbs beef chuck or shank, cut into large 2-inch cubes
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 shallots (hành tím), thinly sliced
  • 1 piece ginger (gừng)
  • 3 stalks lemongrass (sả), bruised and cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut water (nước dừa)
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 star anise (hoa hồi)
  • 2 tablespoons annatto oil (dầu điều) or 1 tablespoon annatto seeds fried in oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (nước mắm)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder (ngũ vị hương)
  • 2 large carrots, cut into thick chunks
  • 1 daikon radish, cut into thick chunks (optional)
  • Fresh herbs — Thai basil, cilantro, sawtooth herb (ngò gai)
  • Bean sprouts and lime wedges, for serving
  • French bread (bánh mì) or rice noodles (phở or hủ tiếu)

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Cut the beef into large chunks — don't go too small, they'll shrink as they braise. Pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Heat annatto oil in a large heavy pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Fry the sliced shallots, ginger, and lemongrass in the oil until fragrant, about 3–4 minutes.
  4. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds. Then add the beef in batches, searing on all sides until browned. Don't skip this step — the sear locks in flavor.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth and coconut water. Add tomato paste, fish sauce, sugar, five-spice powder, cinnamon sticks, and star anise. Stir everything together and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low. Cover and let it simmer gently for 1.5–2 hours. The beef should be tender enough to break apart with chopsticks but still hold its shape.
  7. Add the carrots (and daikon if using) in the last 30 minutes of cooking so they're tender but not mushy.
  8. Taste and adjust — more fish sauce for salt, sugar to balance. The broth should be rich, aromatic, and slightly sweet from the coconut water.
  9. Serve in deep bowls with French bread for dipping, or ladle over rice noodles. Top with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and a squeeze of lime.
