Xôi Gà

Sticky Rice with Chicken

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
Overnight + 30 min
Cook Time
1 hour
Servings
6–8
Category
Xôi

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

Xôi · Sticky Rice

  • 3 cups glutinous rice (gạo nếp), rinsed well
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut cream (nước cốt dừa)
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Gà · Chicken

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon MSG (bột ngọt)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder (ngũ vị hương)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 shallots (hành tím), minced
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper

Đồ ăn kèm · Toppings

  • Fried shallots (hành phi)
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Nước mắm pha dipping sauce

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. The night before: wash the glutinous rice and soak overnight in coconut cream.
  2. Marinate the chicken: mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, MSG, sugar, five-spice powder, garlic, shallots, and black pepper. Coat the chicken thighs thoroughly. Marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight alongside the rice.
  3. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Arrange the chicken thighs skin-side up on a baking sheet lined with foil. Bake for about 1 hour, until the skin is golden and crispy and the meat is tender and pulling away from the bone.
  4. While the chicken bakes, steam the sticky rice: drain any excess liquid, spread the rice in a steamer basket lined with cheesecloth. Steam over boiling water for 25–30 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until the rice is translucent and tender but still chewy.
  5. When the chicken is done, let it rest for a few minutes, then chop it into pieces — bone and all, the Vietnamese way — or shred it if you prefer.
  6. To serve, mound the sticky rice on a plate, arrange the chicken on top, and scatter with fried shallots and fresh cilantro. Drizzle with any pan juices from the chicken.
