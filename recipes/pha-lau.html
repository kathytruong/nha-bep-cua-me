Phá Lấu

Vietnamese Offal Stew

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
45 min
Cook Time
2 hours
Servings
6–8
Category
Kho

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork or beef tripe (bao tử), cleaned
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda (for cleaning)
  • 2 tablespoons salt (for cleaning)
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 shallots (hành tím), sliced
  • 2 teaspoons five-spice powder (ngũ vị hương)
  • 2 star anise (hoa hồi)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk (nước cốt dừa)
  • 2 cups chicken broth or water
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (nước mắm)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 stalks lemongrass (sả), bruised
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fresh chili peppers, for garnish

Để ăn kèm · To Serve

  • French bread (bánh mì) — for dipping
  • Steamed rice
  • Fresh herbs — Thai basil, cilantro
  • Hoisin sauce and Sriracha

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Cleaning the tripe is the most important step — Mẹ was very particular about this. Rub the tripe thoroughly with salt and baking soda, scrubbing every fold and crevice. Rinse under cold running water. Repeat 2–3 times until there is no smell.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the tripe and boil for 10 minutes. Drain and rinse again under cold water. This double-cleaning ensures the tripe is perfectly clean and tender.
  3. Cut the cleaned tripe into bite-sized strips, about 2 inches long and ½ inch wide.
  4. Heat oil in a heavy pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic and shallots until golden and fragrant. Add five-spice powder, star anise, cinnamon stick, and lemongrass. Stir for 1 minute — the kitchen will smell incredible.
  5. Add the tripe strips and toss to coat in the aromatics. Pour in the soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  6. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and let it braise gently for 1.5–2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the tripe is very tender and the sauce is rich and creamy.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning — the broth should be rich, fragrant, and slightly sweet from the coconut milk, with warmth from the five-spice.
  8. Serve hot in deep bowls with crusty French bread for dipping into the coconut-spiced broth. Mẹ's phá lấu was a weekend dish — the slow braising filled the house with the most comforting aroma, and everyone would keep coming back to the pot for one more bowl.
