Chuối Nếp Nướng

Grilled Sticky Rice with Banana

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
30 min + soak
Cook Time
40 min
Servings
8–10
Category
Chè

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 2–3 cups sticky rice (gạo nếp), soaked at least 4 hours or overnight
  • 8–10 ripe bananas (chuối xiêm or chuối sứ), not too soft
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut cream
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Banana leaves, cut into rectangles for wrapping
  • Kitchen string or toothpicks for securing

Nước cốt dừa lá dứa · Pandan Coconut Sauce

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut cream
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 3–4 pandan leaves, tied in a knot
  • 1 tablespoon tapioca starch mixed with 2 tablespoons water

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Drain the soaked sticky rice. In a pot or rice cooker, cook the sticky rice with the coconut cream, sugar, and salt instead of plain water. The rice should absorb all the coconut and become rich, fragrant, and slightly sticky — not mushy. Stir once halfway through to distribute the coconut evenly.
  2. While the rice cooks, prepare the banana leaves. Wilt them briefly over a gas flame or dip in hot water — this makes them pliable and easy to fold without tearing. Cut into rectangles about 8 by 10 inches. Mẹ would run each leaf quickly over the stove burner, just until it turned a deeper green and became soft.
  3. Peel the bananas. If they are large, you can cut them in half lengthwise. The bananas should be ripe and sweet but still firm enough to hold their shape during grilling.
  4. Lay a banana leaf rectangle on your work surface, shiny side up. Spread a layer of coconut sticky rice (about ¼ inch thick) in the center. Place a banana on top, then cover with more sticky rice, pressing gently to enclose the banana completely. Fold the banana leaf around the package — bottom up, top down, then fold in the sides. Secure with kitchen string or toothpicks.
  5. Make the pandan coconut sauce: In a small saucepan, heat the coconut cream with sugar, salt, and pandan leaves. Bring to a gentle simmer. Add the tapioca starch slurry and stir until the sauce thickens slightly. Remove the pandan leaves. Keep warm.
  6. Grill the wrapped packages over medium coals or on a grill pan over medium heat, about 8–10 minutes per side. Turn them carefully. The banana leaf will char in spots — that's good, it adds a wonderful smoky fragrance. You'll know they're done when the leaf is nicely charred on both sides and the package feels firm when pressed.
  7. Unwrap and slice each package crosswise into rounds, revealing the layers of sticky rice and banana inside. Serve warm with the pandan coconut sauce drizzled over top.

Mẹ's tip

The best chuối nếp nướng comes from patient grilling over low, steady heat. Too hot and the outside burns before the inside warms through. Mẹ would set up the charcoal grill in the backyard and turn each one carefully, never rushing. The smell of charred banana leaves and sweet coconut rice drifting through the house meant something special was coming. If you don't have a charcoal grill, a cast-iron pan works well too — press the packages down gently with a spatula as they cook.
