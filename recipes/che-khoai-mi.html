Chè Khoai Mì Nướng

Baked Cassava Cake

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
30 min + soak
Cook Time
45–60 min
Servings
8–10
Category
Chè

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 2 pounds cassava (khoai mì), peeled
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or coconut oil
  • Vanilla extract (optional)
  • Aluminum foil for covering

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Peel the cassava and soak it overnight in cold water. This softens the flesh and removes any bitterness. Mẹ always insisted on the overnight soak — "không ngâm thì cứng lắm" (if you don't soak it, it'll be too tough).
  2. Drain the cassava and squeeze out as much water as possible. You can grate it finely or pulse it in a food processor until it reaches a coarse, crumbly texture. Squeeze again through cheesecloth if it still feels very wet.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the grated cassava, sugar, coconut milk, salt, and melted butter. Mix and knead everything together until well combined. The batter should be thick but moist, almost like a dense cookie dough.
  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking pan or line it with parchment paper.
  5. Pour the cassava mixture into the prepared pan and press it down evenly. Cover tightly with aluminum foil — this traps the steam and helps the inside cook through before the top browns.
  6. Bake covered for 30 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake for another 15–30 minutes until the top turns golden brown and the edges begin to caramelize. The cake should feel firm when you press the center.
  7. Let it cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before cutting. The texture firms up as it cools. Cut into squares or diamond shapes and serve at room temperature.

Mẹ's tip

The trick to a good chè khoai mì nướng is squeezing out enough water from the grated cassava. Too wet and it won't hold together; too dry and it'll be crumbly. Mẹ would test it by pressing a handful — it should clump but not drip. She loved when the top got nice and golden, almost like a caramel crust.
