Yogurt Việt Nam

Vietnamese Homemade Yogurt

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
8–10 hours
Servings
10–12 jars
Category
Chè

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can boiling water (use the empty condensed milk can to measure)
  • 2 cans fresh milk (use the same can to measure — whole milk works best)
  • ½ cup plain yogurt with live cultures (yogurt starter / men yogurt)
  • Small glass jars or ceramic cups for setting

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Pour the entire can of sweetened condensed milk into a large mixing bowl or pot. Boil water and measure out 1 can's worth (use the empty condensed milk can). Pour the boiling water into the condensed milk and stir well until completely combined. The hot water dissolves the thick condensed milk and makes it smooth.
  2. Measure out 2 cans of fresh milk using the same can. Add the fresh milk to the mixture and stir everything together. The mixture should feel warm but not hot to the touch — about the temperature of a warm bath. This is important: if it's too hot, it will kill the yogurt cultures. Mẹ would test it by dipping her pinky in — "ấm tay là được" (warm to the hand is just right).
  3. Add the yogurt starter and stir gently until evenly distributed. Don't whisk vigorously — just stir enough to blend it in. The live cultures in the starter yogurt are what will transform the milk into yogurt overnight.
  4. Pour the mixture into small glass jars or ceramic cups, filling each about three-quarters full. Mẹ used the little glass jars that Vietnamese yogurt comes in — she saved every single one.
  5. Prepare a hot water bath: Fill a large, deep pot or insulated container with hot water (about 140–150°F / 60–65°C). Place the filled jars carefully into the water — the water should come up to about the level of the yogurt inside the jars, but not over the tops.
  6. Cover the pot with a lid and wrap the whole thing in a thick towel or blanket to keep the warmth in. Let it sit undisturbed for 8–10 hours, or overnight. The warmth incubates the cultures and lets them work their magic. Don't peek or jostle it — patience is everything here.
  7. After 8–10 hours, the yogurt should be set — it will jiggle slightly when you tilt the jar but hold its shape. Carefully remove the jars from the water bath and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before eating. The yogurt firms up more as it chills.
  8. Serve cold, straight from the jar. Vietnamese yogurt is meant to be sweet and creamy right out of the jar — no toppings needed, though fresh fruit is always welcome.

Mẹ's tip

The can is your measuring cup — that's the whole trick to this recipe. One can condensed milk, one can boiling water, two cans fresh milk. No need for fancy equipment or thermometers. Mẹ made yogurt every week, lining up those little glass jars on the counter like soldiers. She'd always save a jar from each batch to use as the starter for the next one. "Để dành một hũ làm giống" — save one jar for the next generation. She thought that was very clever, and honestly, it is.
