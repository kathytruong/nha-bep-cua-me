Cua Rang Me

Tamarind Crab

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
30 min
Cook Time
20 min
Servings
4
Category
Xào

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 2 whole live crabs (about 2 lbs each), cleaned
  • ½ cup tamarind paste (me chua), dissolved in 1 cup warm water and strained
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 shallots (hành tím), minced
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (nước mắm)
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • ½ teaspoon MSG (bột ngọt)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (for dusting crab)

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Clean the crabs thoroughly. Remove the top shell and carefully scoop out the roe (gạch cua) — set it aside, this is precious. Cut the crab body into quarters. Crack the claws with the back of a heavy knife so the sauce can penetrate.
  2. Lightly dust the crab pieces with cornstarch. This helps them sear nicely and thickens the sauce later.
  3. Heat oil in a large wok over high heat until smoking. Sear the crab pieces for 2–3 minutes, turning once, until the shells turn bright orange. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same wok, sauté garlic and shallots until golden and fragrant. Pour in the strained tamarind water, fish sauce, sugar, and MSG. Stir and let it bubble for 2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  5. Return the crab to the wok. Toss to coat in the sauce. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it cook for 8–10 minutes, turning the pieces occasionally so every side soaks up that tangy-sweet sauce.
  6. Spoon the reserved roe on top in the last minute of cooking — it melts into the sauce and makes it impossibly rich.
  7. Serve immediately on a large platter. This is a hands-on dish — Mẹ always put a big roll of paper towels on the table and let everyone dig in. Best eaten with warm French bread to soak up every drop of sauce.
