Đậu Câu Nước Dừa

Coconut Water Jelly

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
15 min + chill
Servings
6–8
Category
Chè

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 1 fresh coconut — use the water inside (nước dừa tươi), about 1–1.5 cups
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut cream (nước cốt dừa)
  • ¾ cup sugar (đường), or to taste
  • 1 packet agar-agar powder (bột rau câu), about 10–12g
  • Pinch of salt

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Crack open the fresh coconut and pour the water into a pot through a strainer to catch any shell fragments. Mẹ always used fresh coconut water — she said the sweetness is different from the boxed kind, more delicate and floral.
  2. Add the cup of water and the sugar to the pot with the coconut water. Sprinkle in the agar-agar powder and a pinch of salt. Let it sit for a few minutes so the agar-agar begins to soften.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring constantly. The agar-agar needs to dissolve completely — keep stirring for about 2–3 minutes after it boils. If there are still granules, it won't set properly.
  4. Reduce the heat and stir in the coconut cream. Mix well until the liquid is smooth and creamy, with a uniform milky-white color. Taste and add more sugar if you like — it should be gently sweet, not overpowering.
  5. Pour the mixture into a mold or a glass dish. Mẹ sometimes used individual small cups so each person got their own portion. Let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until fully set, about 2–3 hours.
  6. Once set, the jelly should be soft and wobbly with a silky texture — firm enough to hold its shape but tender on the tongue. Cut into pieces or unmold onto a plate. Serve chilled on its own or with fresh fruit. The coconut flavor should be clean and pure — a simple, cool dessert for a hot day.
