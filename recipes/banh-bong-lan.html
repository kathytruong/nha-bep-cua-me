Bánh Bông Lan

Vietnamese Sponge Cake

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
20 min
Cook Time
35–40 min
Servings
8–10
Category
Bánh

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 5 large eggs, separated
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 tablespoons cream (heavy cream or whipping cream)
  • 1 tablespoon instant coffee, dissolved in 1 tablespoon hot water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons bột cream (cream of tartar)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line the bottom of a 9-inch round cake pan with parchment paper. Do not grease the sides — the batter needs to cling to the sides to rise properly.
  2. Separate the eggs. Place the whites in a large, clean bowl and the yolks in a medium bowl.
  3. Beat the egg yolks with half the sugar until thick and pale, about 3 minutes. Add the oil, cream, dissolved coffee, and vanilla. Mix until well combined.
  4. Gently fold in the sifted flour, mixing just until no dry streaks remain. Do not overmix.
  5. In the other bowl, beat the egg whites with the cream of tartar and a pinch of salt until foamy. Gradually add the remaining sugar, beating until stiff, glossy peaks form.
  6. Fold one-third of the egg whites into the yolk batter to lighten it. Then gently fold in the remaining whites in two additions, turning the bowl as you fold. Be patient — keeping the air in is what makes the cake fluffy.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles.
  8. Bake at 350°F for 35–40 minutes, until the top is golden and a skewer inserted in the center comes out clean.
  9. Immediately invert the cake pan onto a wire rack and let it cool completely upside down. This keeps the cake from collapsing. Once cool, run a knife along the edges and unmold.
  10. Slice and serve with tea.
