Dưa Mắm

Fermented Vegetables

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
20 min
Cook Time
1–2 days (ferment)
Servings
1 jar
Category
Đồ Chua

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 1 lb cucumbers (dưa leo), firm and fresh
  • 2 tablespoons salt (muối)
  • Water, enough to submerge
  • 2–3 tablespoons fish sauce (nước mắm)
  • 1–2 tablespoons sugar (đường)

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Cut each cucumber in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scrape out the seeds — Mẹ said removing the seeds keeps the dưa from getting watery and soft during fermentation.
  2. Pack the cucumber halves tightly into a clean glass jar. Really press them in — the tighter they're packed, the better they ferment because there's less air inside.
  3. Dissolve the salt in water to make a brine. Pour over the cucumbers until they are completely submerged. Place a small plate or weight on top to keep the cucumbers pressed under the liquid.
  4. Cover loosely and let ferment at room temperature for 1–2 days. The cucumbers will turn from bright green to a yellowish olive color — that's how you know the fermentation is working.
  5. After fermenting, drain the cucumbers. Rinse lightly and let them drain well in a colander.
  6. Transfer the drained cucumbers to a clean jar. Add the sugar and fish sauce, tossing gently to coat. The fish sauce gives it that deep, savory funk that makes dưa mắm different from regular pickles — it's bolder, more complex.
  7. Let the flavors meld for a few hours in the refrigerator before serving. Dưa mắm is wonderful alongside canh chua, cơm tấm, or any rice dish that needs a salty, tangy bite on the side.
