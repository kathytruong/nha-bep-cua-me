Muối Dưa Giá

Pickled Bean Sprouts

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
3 days (ferment)
Servings
1 jar
Category
Đồ Chua

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 1 lb bean sprouts (giá), washed and drained
  • 1 bunch chives (hẹ), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 carrot (cà rốt), peeled and julienned
  • 2 tablespoons salt (muối)
  • A small pinch of alum (phèn chua) — this keeps the bean sprouts crunchy
  • Water, enough to submerge

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Wash the bean sprouts and pick off any brown tails if you have the patience — Mẹ always did, but she said it's fine to skip if you're in a hurry. Drain well.
  2. Cut the chives into 2-inch pieces. Peel and julienne the carrot into thin matchsticks.
  3. Toss the bean sprouts, chives, and carrot together in a large bowl with the salt and the pinch of alum. Mix everything gently with your hands.
  4. Pack the mixture tightly into a clean glass jar. Press down firmly — Mẹ used the back of a spoon to really compress it. The vegetables should release some liquid as you press.
  5. Add enough water to just cover the vegetables. Place a weight on top (a small plate or a plastic bag filled with water works) to keep everything submerged.
  6. Cover loosely and leave at room temperature for about 3 days, until the dưa giá tastes pleasantly sour. It should be tangy and crunchy — if it smells off or gets slimy, the jar wasn't clean enough.
  7. Transfer to the refrigerator once it's sour enough. Serve alongside bánh xèo, cơm tấm, or any grilled meat. The bright crunch of dưa giá is the perfect contrast to rich, savory dishes.
