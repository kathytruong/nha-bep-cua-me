Bánh Tôm Chiên

Fried Shrimp Cakes

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
15 min
Cook Time
15 min
Servings
4–6
Category
Chiên

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

Bột · Batter

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder (bột nghệ) — for golden color
  • 2 cups sparkling water or beer (Mẹ preferred beer for extra crispiness)

Tôm · Shrimp

  • 1 lb medium shrimp, peeled and deveined (leave tails on for presentation)
  • Oil, for deep frying

Để ăn kèm · To Serve

  • Fresh lettuce leaves
  • Fresh herbs — mint, perilla (tía tô), cilantro
  • Pickled carrots and daikon (đồ chua)
  • Nước chấm dipping sauce

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Make the batter: whisk together flour, beaten eggs, salt, and turmeric until smooth. Slowly pour in the sparkling water or beer, stirring gently. The batter should be thin enough to coat the back of a spoon — lighter than pancake batter. Don't overmix — a few lumps are fine and actually help with crispiness.
  2. Let the batter rest for 10 minutes. The carbonation in the sparkling water or beer creates tiny bubbles that make the coating impossibly light and crispy when fried.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pot or wok to 350°F. You need enough oil to submerge the shrimp — at least 2 inches deep.
  4. Dip each shrimp into the batter, letting the excess drip off. Carefully lower into the hot oil. Fry 3–4 shrimp at a time — don't crowd them or the temperature drops and they get greasy instead of crispy.
  5. Fry for 3–4 minutes, turning once, until the batter is puffed up, golden, and crispy. The turmeric gives them a beautiful sunny color. Drain on a wire rack.
  6. Serve immediately — these are best eaten hot and crackling. Wrap each shrimp cake in a lettuce leaf with fresh herbs and pickled vegetables, then dip in nước chấm. Mẹ made these as a special treat, and they always disappeared faster than she could fry them.
