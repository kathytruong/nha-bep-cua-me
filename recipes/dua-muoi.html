Dưa Muối

Vietnamese Pickled Vegetables

From Mẹ — Oanh

Prep Time
15 min
Cook Time
3–4 days (ferment)
Servings
1 large jar
Category
Đồ Chua

Nguyên liệu · Ingredients

  • 7 green papayas or assorted vegetables (mustard greens, cabbage, or radish work too)
  • 4 tablespoons salt (muối)
  • Enough water to submerge the vegetables
  • 1–2 tablespoons sugar (for the second stage)

Cách làm · Instructions

  1. Wash and prepare the vegetables. If using green papaya, peel and cut into bite-sized pieces. For mustard greens or cabbage, separate the leaves and cut into manageable chunks. Let them wilt in the sun for a few hours if you can — Mẹ said this helps them stay crunchy after pickling.
  2. Dissolve the salt in water to make a brine. The water should taste noticeably salty. Place the vegetables in a clean glass jar or large container and pour the salted water over them until fully submerged.
  3. Press the vegetables down with a plate or heavy bowl to keep them under the brine. Cover loosely — the fermentation needs a little air to breathe.
  4. Let the jar sit at room temperature for 3 days. You'll start to see tiny bubbles — that means the good bacteria are working.
  5. After 3 days, taste the dưa. If it's sour enough for your liking, add a little sugar water to balance the flavor. Mẹ would check it every day after day 3, tasting until it hit that perfect tangy-sweet spot.
  6. Once the pickles reach your desired sourness (usually 3–4 days total), move the jar to the refrigerator to slow the fermentation. They'll keep for a couple of weeks. Serve alongside rice, soup, or braised dishes — dưa muối makes everything better.
