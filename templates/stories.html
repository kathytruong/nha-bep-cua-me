The memories behind the meals

A memory of Mẹ — Oanh

"The pot that never went cold"

Mẹ's phở pot lived on the back burner for two days at a time. She'd start it before anyone woke up — bones from the Asian grocery, a knob of ginger charred directly on the gas flame, star anise she kept in a tin above the fridge.

The house would smell like broth by the time we got home from school. She never wrote anything down. "Nước trong là được," she'd say — when the broth is clear, it's ready. She'd skim it every hour, patient in a way none of us inherited.

We'd eat it for dinner, and then again for breakfast. The pot just kept going. When someone came over — a neighbor, a cousin passing through — there was always enough. That was the rule. There was always enough.
