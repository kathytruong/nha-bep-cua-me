About this kitchen

This isn't a food blog. There are no ads, no affiliate links, no SEO-optimized headings. It's a family recipe site — small on purpose.

It's for the cousins — and for our kids, who will one day want to know what their grandmother's phở tasted like.

These are the recipes of our mother, Oanh, and her sisters — the Dì's who fed us every weekend and taught us, whether we were paying attention or not.

Our family is Northern Vietnamese. That shapes everything here — clear broths, restrained spice, fish sauce that knew its place. Mẹ didn't believe in shortcuts. The broth took two days because it took two days.

We grew up speaking English at school and Vietnamese at home — especially in the kitchen. When we talk about food, it slips out in Vietnamese: phở, nước mắm, mặn, ngọt, chua, and so many more. This is the language of our table.

Most of these recipes were never written down. Mẹ cooked by feel — a handful of this, enough of that, "you'll know when it's ready." We've done our best to translate that into measurements for some, but the truth is: you might need to call your Mẹ.

If you found this site and you're not family — welcome anyway. Cook something. Tell us how it turned out.

We built this because we don't want to forget. Because one day our kids will stand in their own kitchens and want to make something that tastes like home. And we want them to have somewhere to start.
